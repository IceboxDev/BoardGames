# Parks Passion Card Art

10 passion cards — one per `PassionId` (`adventure`, `birdwatching`, `botany`, `collecting`, `forestry`, `kayaking`, `mountaineering`, `rock-climbing`, `swimming`, `wildlife`). At game start each player is dealt two passion options and chooses one in a full-screen overlay. The art is the entire card face — title, descriptive text, and selection chrome are composited by the UI on top of the image.

These illustrations should feel like a coherent set of national-park screenprint posters: each one a single, confident image of the activity (not a literal scene-painting, not a photo, not realism). They will be shown large in the overlay and small as a tag on the player's panel for the rest of the game, so the silhouette and palette must read at both sizes.

---

## Shared Card Art Style Directive

> Topographic screenprinted national-park poster. Bold carved-looking shape masses, engraved contour and stipple linework, heroic simplification with one or two dominant landforms or motifs sweeping across the frame. Limited harmonious earth-and-sky ink palette: dusty pine green, deep forest teal, warm ochre, sandstone orange, rust red, lake blue, glacier blue, sage, cream, muted mustard, soft clay pink. No black-heavy contrast — all darks read as deep teal, deep forest, or rust. Mid-century American park poster romanticism (think WPA / Anderson Design Group / Eyvind Earle hybrid) with modern tabletop graphic clarity. Reverent, calm, wholesome, celebratory mood. Gentle paper grain and subtle screenprint misregistration texture; no photorealism, no airbrush gradients, no harsh black outlines, no digital glow. Composition is wide cinematic landscape — horizon-respecting, layered foreground/midground/background bands, key motifs placed for landscape balance (rule-of-thirds, off-center heroes). No text, no numbers, no card borders, no UI elements. Landscape 16:9 aspect ratio, 1280×720 minimum.
