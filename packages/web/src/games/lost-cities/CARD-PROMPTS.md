# Lost Cities Card Art

60 individual card illustrations (12 per expedition color) telling a cohesive story of discovery across five expeditions. Each card shows a chapter in the journey — from bartering for resources (wager cards) through exploration (2-8) to the grand reveal of a real-world legendary location (card 10).

The UI composites card frame elements (thick black border, black header with expedition-colored numbers in both top corners) on top of these illustrations — the prompts generate **only the scenic art**.

---

## Shared Card Art Style Directive

> Cinematic digital illustration with polished, clean rendering and rich atmospheric depth. Luminous lighting with dramatic color grading and soft environmental haze. Detailed, immersive environments with a sense of scale and wonder, inspired by premium tabletop game art (Everdell, Mysterium, Dixit) and fantasy concept art. Warm, inviting tones with cinematic contrast. No visible brushstrokes, no paint textures. Sharp architectural details softened by atmospheric perspective. No text, no numbers, no card borders, no UI elements. Portrait 2:3 aspect ratio, 512×768 minimum.

---

## Recurring Characters

Three explorers appear consistently across all 60 images. Include these identifying features whenever a character appears:

- **Dr. Amara Osei** — Expedition leader, early 50s, silver-streaked dark hair in a loose bun, olive field jacket, leather-bound journal always in hand.
- **Felix Navarro** — Cartographer and navigator, late 20s, tousled auburn hair, rolled-up sleeves, brass compass and instruments.
- **Lian Zhou** — Linguist and archaeologist, mid-30s, round spectacles, practical field gear, carries ancient texts and translation notebooks.

## Narrative Arc

| Card    | Chapter    | Beat                                                                     |
| ------- | ---------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Wager 1 | Intel      | Bartering goods for information — maps, coordinates, ancient knowledge   |
| Wager 2 | Provisions | Exchanging valuables for equipment — tools, gear, supplies               |
| Wager 3 | Passage    | Trading for transport and access — a guide, vessel, or safe passage      |
| 2       | First Light | First view of the vast landscape, tiny figures, enormous scale          |
| 3       | The Old Road | Following traces of an ancient path                                   |
| 4       | Fragments  | First tangible evidence of the lost civilization                         |
| 5       | Camp       | Base camp established to study findings                                  |
| 6       | The Passage | Discovery of a hidden entrance leading deeper                           |
| 7       | Trial      | The expedition's darkest hour — a dangerous crossing or disaster         |
| 8       | Revelation | Having survived, the team sees something extraordinary                   |
| 9       | The Gates  | Standing before the actual entrance to the lost city                     |
| 10      | The Wonder | The grand reveal — a real-world legendary location in its ancient glory  |
